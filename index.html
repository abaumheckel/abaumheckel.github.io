Audrey Baumheckel



I'm Audrey, a mathematics PhD student at the University of Nebraska – Lincoln advised by Kristen Amman and Yvonne Lai. My research interests lie in mathematics education, especially as it relates to how we can encourage students to think critically within the mathematics classroom and in the rest of their lives.



Before becoming a Cornhusker, I recieved my B.S. at Fresno State, where I was a scholar in the Smittcamp Family Honors College and affiliated with the Mentoring Math Scholars for Success program. While getting my undergraduate degree, I had the pleasure of being mentored by Tom Forgacs in two projects in number theory, by Rolland Trapp at an REU in knot theory, and by Carmen Caprau for my undergraduate thesis in knot theory. I then continued to get my M.S. at Fresno State, where I was affiliated with the Bolstering the Advancement of Masters in Mathematics program. I was greatly benefitted by my Master's thesis mentor, Oscar Vega. The summer before going to UNL, I also had the amazing opportunity to go to the Edge program, which was a wonderful and life-changing 4 weeks in which I met some of the most amazing people ever.
